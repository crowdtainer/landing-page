Meet your farmers.


Although our vision is to eventually bootstrap an open marketplace for farmers, roasters, and customers to find each other, we need to take one step at a time and find out if programmable money can work for our own farms first. Here's a chance to learn more about them and the history behind this cup of coffee.


Santa Inacia Farm

Rosiane Alencar and David Boyko

What used to be a sugar cane plantation gave origin to an extremely healthy soil.

The favorable climate and altitude from 1100 to 1450 meters above sea level makes the right combination to cultivate and produce a very special coffee.

Location: Pedralva, Minas Gerais, Brazil.
Varieties: Red Catuai, Yellow Catuai, Catuaí, Mundo Novo and Yellow Bourbon.
Process: Washed and dry pulping.

Equipped with dry mill, screen size, weight, and color sorting machines, coffee is carefully hand picked, dried, and processed at the farm itself - all contributing to the freshness and quality of the coffee bean.

Greenhouses help dry the coffee using the sun's heat. Despite slower, it is more gentle and therefore helps the beans keep their natural sweet taste.

Coffee species Red Catuai, Yellow Catuai, Catucai, Mundo Novo and Yellow Bourbom are cultivated in this farm, employing over 20 families.

Santa Teresa Farm

Isley de Alencar Jr. & Family

Years of experience come into play to make the best out of each coffee grain, and in that regard, coffee tradition is in the family as far as they can tell about the past - Alencar is a 3rd generation family-run coffee producer.

Location: Pedralva, Minas Gerais, Brazil.
Altitude: 1300 meters above sea level.
Varieties: Yellow and Red Catuaí.
Process: Washed and dry pulping.

Santa Teresa is surrounded by a deep native forest. In this forest there are wild bees that helps with polinising the coffee trees - resulting in a very productive and healthy harvest.

Banana trees are planted intertwined with the coffee trees, providing natural shade conditions that optimises the amount of sun light reaching the coffee tree, as well as taking care of the environment, keeping the local biodiversity and birds habitat healthy.
